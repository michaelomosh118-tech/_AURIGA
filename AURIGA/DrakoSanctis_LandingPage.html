How It Works

AurigaNavi uses a physical 20×20cm colored reference square placed on the ground during a one-time 10-point calibration. The TruePath™ engine stores the apparent pixel width of that square at each known distance, building a monotone cubic interpolation curve. At runtime, a virtual fiducial is scaled to match the ground plane at the base of any detected obstacle — matching that scale gives the real-world distance without any depth sensor or LiDAR.

Core Capabilities
  • Sub-20cm distance accuracy from 0.5m to 5m
  • 3-column spatial scan (Left 25%, Center, Right 75%)
  • SkyShield™ suspended obstacle detection with clearance height
  • Stereo audio bearing — beeps pan left/right with the obstacle
  • Proximity-scaled haptic feedback (intensity by distance)
  • DrakoVoice™ TTS: "Wall, 1.2 metres, 10 degrees left"
  • Auto-flash in low light with 12-frame debounce
  • GhostAnchor™ stabilisation for tremor compensation

03
Print & Prepare Your Training Marker
TruePath™ requires a one-time calibration using a physical colored square. This is the "ground ruler" the engine uses to measure all future distances. You only do this once per device.
  • 3.1 Print a solid-colored square exactly 20cm × 20cm on plain white paper. Use bright red or lime green — these colors are rarely found in natural environments and give the best detection confidence.
  • 3.2 Laminate it or place it in a clear plastic sleeve. The surface must be flat — any curl or crease reduces detection confidence.
  • 3.3 Place a length of tape or rope on the ground in a straight line from where you will stand. Mark distances at 0.5m, 1.0m, 1.5m, 2.0m, 2.5m, 3.0m, 3.5m, 4.0m, 4.5m, and 5.0m using chalk or tape markers.
ℹ The colored square is your permanent calibration tool. Keep it — you will need it if you reset the app or switch devices.
04
Run the 10-Point TruePath™ Calibration
This process takes approximately 5 minutes and is the most important step. The accuracy of every future distance reading depends on how carefully you complete this calibration. Do it indoors in good lighting for best results.
  • 4.1 In the app, tap the Calibrate tab in the bottom navigation bar. Tap BEGIN CALIBRATION.
  • 4.2 Place your colored square flat on the ground at the 0.5m mark. Hold the phone steady, pointed at the square, at a natural carrying angle (approximately 30° downward from horizontal).
  • 4.3 Watch the targeting reticle on screen. When it turns green and shows "✓ Point Locked", the engine has detected the square with sufficient confidence (>70%).
  • 4.4 The distance field will show 0.5m. Confirm it is correct, then tap CONFIRM POINT. You will feel a double haptic pulse confirming the point was saved.
  • 4.5 Move the square to the 1.0m mark and repeat. Continue through all 10 distances up to 5.0m. The progress bar at the top shows "X / 10".
  • 4.6 When the screen shows "✓ Calibration Complete", tap RETURN TO NAVIGATION. Your calibration data is saved permanently to this device.
⚠ Do not skip or rush points. A poorly captured point creates inaccurate distance readings at that range. If the reticle does not lock within 10 seconds, check that the square is in good light and fully flat on the floor.

  • 6.5 In dark environments, the flashlight activates automatically after 12 consecutive low-light frames.

How It Works

AurigaSentinel uses the same TruePath™ spatial geometry engine as AurigaNavi but reorients its purpose from navigation to safety monitoring. It detects human presence using aspect ratio geometry — a person has a height-to-width ratio consistently above 1.8:1. When a standing person suddenly becomes horizontal, the aspect ratio inverts, triggering a fall detection event. No faces, no biometrics, no video stored — purely geometric metadata.

Core Capabilities
  • Fall detection via aspect-ratio inversion geometry
  • Presence detection without biometric data collection
  • Vectorized JSON event packets (0.5KB vs 5MB video)
  • Multi-node mesh via LoRa, Wi-Fi 6, or DrakoLink P2P
  • Low-power frame differencing mode (battery optimised)
  • GDPR-compliant by design — no images transmitted
  • Caregiver alert via push notification on event trigger

Deployment Environments

AurigaSentinel is designed for residential care facilities, private homes with elderly or mobility-impaired residents, and commercial spaces requiring non-intrusive occupancy awareness. The CLASSIC variant mounts visibly as a deterrent. The CAMALEÓ variant integrates into architectural elements or outdoor fixtures with solar power for off-grid mesh nodes. Both variants run identically on any Android device or Raspberry Pi with a camera module.

SDK Overview

AurigaAero exposes the full TruePath™ spatial geometry engine as a developer SDK targeting drone flight controllers, robotic navigation stacks, and autonomous vehicle systems. The SDK provides real-time obstacle distance, bearing, and clearance height from any camera feed — no LiDAR, no depth sensor, no specialist hardware required. It compiles to ARMv8 (Raspberry Pi / Jetson Nano), ESP32-S3, and STM32 Cortex-M4 targets.

How It Works

AurigaIndustrial mounts a low-cost Android tablet or ESP32-CAM module on a forklift, pallet jack, or warehouse robot. The TruePath™ engine measures real-world distance to obstacles in the vehicle's path continuously. SkyShield™ simultaneously monitors the overhead clearance zone — detecting low-hanging beams, racks, and suspended hazards that operators cannot see from the cab. Alerts are visual (screen), audible (beep), and haptic (vibration on attached wristband).
